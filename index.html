Intro

Hey, I'm Tabasum! a researcher working on the field of Gravitational and Black hole physics along with Celestial holography.

Currently I am working under the supervision of Dr. Nabamita Banerjee at Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Bhopal, Madhy Pradesh, India. Here is our research group at IISER, Bhopal.

Research

The direct detection of the gravitational waves set up a platform for the scientific community in GW astronomy to investigate different aspects of gravity theories including the major physical effect called as Gravitational Memory effect. This will help us in understanding the properties of black hole spacetimes and gravitational waves. The practical memory computation has been accounted for all phases of the binary black hole coalescence and the peak sensitivity of LIGO and LISA detectors might capture the effect of memory's rise time during the merger and ringdown phases of a BBH. In this direction there have been several implications of GW memory detection in GW150914 through LIGO detector.

The infinite symmetries of the four dimensional asymptotically flat spacetime in both gauge and gravity theories leads us to the triangular relation between memory effect, asymptotic symmetries and soft theorems. Due to the passage of a gravitational radiation, the difference in the initial and final geometries of the spacetime is related by a BMS supertranslation. The Ward identities of this invariance in Quantum Gravity(QG) are expressed as data representing gravitational radiation at null infinity. The recent uncovering of the relation between the on-shell physics of asymptotically flat theories and 2D CFT has proved to be a powerfull tool. The boundary physics is captured by a 2D CFT(CCFT) of the conformal operators on the Celestial Sphere. Celestial amplitudes are of immense importance as an independent entity in itself. The infinite number of symmetries impose constraints on the celestial amplitude via Ward identities which give us the soft theorems. These constraints directly imply constraints on the bulk scattering amplitude. Hence a thorough understanding of celestial amplitudes can uncover new symmetries.

In my recent work we used the celestial conformal field theory formalism to find the asymptotic symmetries algebras of the four dimensional Einstein-Yang-Mills and Einstein-Maxwell theories (Ref). The disjoint study of gravitational memory and asymptotic symmetries will give us more evidences on the general theory of relativity and help us explore far in the past.
