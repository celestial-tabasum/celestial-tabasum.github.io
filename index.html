  • About my research

    The direct detection of the gravitational waves set up a platform for the scientific community in Gravitational Wave Astronomy to investigate different aspects of gravity theories. One of the major physical effect called as Gravitational Memory effect. This will help us in understanding the properties of black hole spacetimes and gravitational waves. The peak sensitivity of LIGO and LISA detectors might capture the effect of memory's rise time during the merger and ringdown phases of a Binary Blackhole(BBH). In this direction there have been several implications of GW memory detection in GW150914 through LIGO detector.

    Theoretically this classical observable effect can be related to the quantum effects like soft theorems and the asymptotic symmetries. I will briefly explain these two quantum phenomeon. This can be easily visible in the diagramatically interpretation of Strominer's IR triangle.

    Let's talk about Soft theorems. This is basically the low energy sector of a scattering process. When we have a scattering process, there are external particles of different energy scales. Now when we make one of the energy of the external particles to zero then we call that particle to be soft particle. But the question is why do we need to do that? The answer to that is in our observable universe the asymptotic observer always sits at the infinities of the spacetime. Which means at the asymptotic boundaries of the spacetime. Hence the information flow will only be possible via the messengers (which is photons in case of electromagnetic interaction in terms of Electromagnetic Waves and gravitons in case of Gravitational interaction in terms of Gravitational Waves). So the soft particles in our case are photons and gravitons. When we study one scattering process we need to find the scattering amplitude. After taking one of the particles to be soft we can write this higher point amplitude in terms of a lower point amplitude multiplied by the universal soft factor. This is one incredible result from the Amplitude community because this theorem is independent of the theory we consider, the structure will be independent of the scattering informations. Isn't this cool!!:)

    The infinite symmetries of the four dimensional asymptotically flat spacetime in both gauge and gravity theories leads us to the triangular relation between memory effect, asymptotic symmetries and soft theorems. Due to the passage of a gravitational radiation, the difference in the initial and final geometries of the spacetime is related by a BMS supertranslation. The Ward identities of this invariance in Quantum Gravity(QG) are expressed as data representing gravitational radiation at null infinity. The recent uncovering of the relation between the on-shell physics of asymptotically flat theories and 2D CFT has proved to be a powerfull tool. The boundary physics is captured by a 2D CFT(CCFT) of the conformal operators on the Celestial Sphere. Celestial amplitudes are of immense importance as an independent entity in itself. The infinite number of symmetries impose constraints on the celestial amplitude via Ward identities which give us the soft theorems. These constraints directly imply constraints on the bulk scattering amplitude. Hence a thorough understanding of celestial amplitudes can uncover new symmetries.

    In my recent work which is with my supervisor Nabamita Banerjee and my collaborator Ranveer Kumar Singh, where we used the celestial conformal field theory formalism to find the asymptotic symmetries algebras of the four dimensional Einstein-Yang-Mills and Einstein-Maxwell theories (Ref). The disjoint study of gravitational memory and asymptotic symmetries will give us more evidences on the general theory of relativity and help us explore far in the past.
